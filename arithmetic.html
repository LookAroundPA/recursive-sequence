Frequently Asked Questions

What is an Arithmetic Sequence?

An arithmetic sequence is a sequence where the difference between consecutive terms is constant. This difference is called the common difference (d).

How to Calculate the nth Term?

The nth term formula is: aₙ = a₁ + (n-1)d, where a₁ is the first term, d is the common difference, and n is the position number.

How to Calculate the Sum?

The sum of the first n terms: Sₙ = n(a₁ + aₙ)/2. Here aₙ is the nth term, a₁ is the first term, and n is the number of terms.
