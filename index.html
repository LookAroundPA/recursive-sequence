Common types of sequences

A sequence is an ordered list of numbers. Its rule tells you how the terms are connected, and recognizing that rule helps you predict later terms or calculate a total.

Arithmetic sequences

An arithmetic sequence changes by the same amount at every step. In 4, 7, 10, 13, the common difference is 3. These sequences model situations with constant additions, such as saving the same amount each week.

Explore the arithmetic sequence calculator

Geometric sequences

A geometric sequence multiplies by the same ratio at every step. In 2, 6, 18, 54, the common ratio is 3. Geometric patterns appear in compound growth, repeated scaling, and decay.

Recursive sequences

A recursive definition describes a term using one or more earlier terms. For example, an arithmetic sequence can be written as a(n) = a(n - 1) + d. A starting value is essential because the recursive rule alone does not determine the whole sequence.

How to use these calculators

  1. Choose the calculator that matches the pattern: constant difference for arithmetic or constant ratio for geometric.
  2. Enter the first term and the common difference or ratio.
  3. Enter the number of terms to generate, or enter a position to find one specific term.
  4. Select the calculation button and review the terms, nth-term value, or finite sum.
  5. Use the formula and examples below each tool to understand how the result was obtained.

Frequently asked questions

How can I tell whether a sequence is arithmetic or geometric?

Check consecutive terms. A constant difference indicates an arithmetic sequence, while a constant nonzero ratio indicates a geometric sequence.

What does “nth term” mean?

The nth term is the value at position n. For example, the fifth term is the term in position 5.

What is the difference between a sequence and a series?

A sequence is an ordered list of terms. A series is the result of adding terms from a sequence.

Can a common difference or ratio be negative?

Yes. A negative difference makes an arithmetic sequence decrease. A negative ratio makes the signs in a geometric sequence alternate.

Do the calculators accept decimal values?

Yes. The first term, common difference, and common ratio can be decimals. Positions and numbers of terms must be positive whole numbers.
